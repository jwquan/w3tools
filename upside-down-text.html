How does upside down text work?

The upsidedown text or FlipText is one of Leetspeak's types and is used in the jargon of the network. It is a kind of secret language and used make text unrecognizable by word filters in chats and forums. The text is rotated 180 ° by first writing backwards, then replacing each character with a character that is as close to a 180 ° as possible. For instance, "Prepostseo" becomes "Ԁɹǝdosʇsǝo"

Only a few characters in the ordinary ASCII character set (b / q, d / p, n / u and 6/9) have correspondences rotated by 180 °, very few are even rotationally symmetrical and need not be changed (z, s, x, o, 0 and 8). The rest of the characters use either special Unicode characters, particularly those of the International Phonetic Alphabet, or only approximately similar characters (e.g. USD-Encoding, w / m).
